Preparing for the Digital SAT requires organization, consistency, and a clear study plan. This checklist helps students track their preparation progress and stay focused on important milestones throughout their SAT journey. For additional SAT preparation resources and university admissions guidance, visit https://www.iasedu.in/.

Before You Begin

Understand the Digital SAT format

Set a target SAT score

Identify your target universities

Create a study schedule

Gather SAT preparation resources

Take a diagnostic test

Reading and Writing Preparation

Review grammar fundamentals

Practice reading comprehension passages

Improve vocabulary in context skills

Learn sentence structure and conventions

Complete weekly reading practice exercises

Analyze mistakes and weak areas

Mathematics Preparation

Review algebra concepts

Practice advanced mathematics questions

Strengthen problem-solving skills

Learn data analysis techniques

Review geometry concepts

Practice calculator and non-calculator strategies

Weekly Preparation Goals

Complete planned study hours

Practice SAT questions daily

Review mistakes from previous exercises

Track progress and performance

Revise previously learned concepts

Practice Test Checklist

Take a full-length practice test every 2–3 weeks

Simulate actual test conditions

Monitor time management

Review incorrect answers

Identify recurring mistakes

Adjust study plans based on results

One Month Before the Exam

Focus on weak topics

Increase mock test frequency

Review important formulas and concepts

Refine test-taking strategies

Improve speed and accuracy

One Week Before the Exam

Complete final revision

Review notes and key concepts

Avoid learning entirely new topics

Maintain a consistent sleep schedule

Prepare exam-day materials

Exam Day Checklist

Confirm test date and location

Carry required identification documents

Arrive early at the test center

Stay calm and focused

Follow time management strategies

Read questions carefully before answering

Post-Exam Checklist

Review score reports

Evaluate performance

Identify improvement opportunities if retaking the test

Research university application requirements

Plan the next steps in the admissions process

Conclusion

A structured checklist helps students stay organized and maintain consistency throughout SAT preparation. Regular practice, performance analysis, and disciplined study habits can significantly improve confidence and exam readiness.
